When did the bakeries started to evolve?

Bakeries started a loooong time ago. Like, about 6000 years back, people in Egypt figured out how to make better bread (they accidentally discovered yeast — the thing that makes bread fluffy). Before that, people were just making flat, hard bread — kinda like boring roti with no swag. Then, in Ancient Greece and Rome, bakeries became a real thing — shops where one person bakes and sells bread to others. Rome even had public bakeries where normal people could buy fresh bread every day. Good bakers in Rome were famous like chefs today.
